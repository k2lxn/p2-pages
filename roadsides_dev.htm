FAST Act Pollinator Roadsides Provision

Encouraging pollinator habitat along roadsides is now the law of the land!

Highway right-of-ways managed by State Departments of Transportation (DOT’ s) represent 17 million acres of opportunity for States to both save money and improve pollinator habitat through Integrated Vegetation Management (IVM), including reduced mowing and strategic plantings of native forbs and grasses.

For the first time ever, encouraging pollinator habitat along our nation’ s roadsides is the law of the land! The President signed the Fixing America’ s Surface Transportation Act (FAST Act)” into law on December 4, 2015.

Section 1415 of the FAST Act directs the Secretary of Transportation to use existing authorities, programs and funding to encourage pollinator habitat efforts through reduced mowing and IVM practices by willing State DOT’ s. In support of this objective, Section 1415 also amends Section 29(a)(1) of Title 23,U.S.C., to make it clear that States can utilize funds from that eligibility account on “habitat, forage, and migratory way stations for Monarch butterflies, other native pollinators, and honey bees.”

States are clearly in the lead in realizing the potential of this new provision. As State DOT representatives showcased during last week’ s “Transportation Leaders Summit: Restoring the Nation’s Pollinator Habitat,” convened by the White House Office of Science and Technology Policy (OSTP), much exciting work is already underway in a number of States in advancing this objective. P2 collaborated with leaders of State Departments of Transportation (DoT’ s), Administration officials and other key stakeholders with the shared objective of advancing efforts to expand and improve pollinator habitat on transportation assets across the Nation.

States should consider related actions that could support and enhance this primary objective, including:

  1. Encourage ‘ cross-pollination’ between your state agencies responsible for transportation, agriculture and natural resources, to facilitate a more coordinated and effective approach.
  2. Encourage and assist county governments and municipalities in pursuing similar practices and objectives along roadsides they manage.
  3. Encourage businesses, NGO’ s, and citizens from all walks of life in your State to understand, recognize and support efforts by your State transportation agency to achieve cost savings and improve pollinator habitat. Consider convening stakeholder forums to facilitate dialog, increase awareness and understanding
  4. Explore ways your State can provide incentives, encouragement and other forms of support to your State transportation agencies to enhance pollinator habitat along roadsides.
  5. Highlight actions to enhance pollinator habitat along roadsides in your 2016 Pollinator Week proclamation (June 20-26) and related events.
