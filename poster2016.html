Trees for Bees

Trees are great food and nesting sources for bees

Bigleaf Maple

Acer macrophyllum (Sapindaceae)

Geographic Distribution:

Western United States and Canada, from British Columbia to California

Pollinators:

Insect pollinators include bees, flies, and beetles.

Habitat:

A large deciduous tree growing in moist woods, forests, and canyons

Mature height:

60-100 feet

IUCN threat status:

Not yet assessed

NatureServe conservation status:

G5 - Secure
